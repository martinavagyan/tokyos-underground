Nishi-Ogikubo

This town is located at the western edge of the 23 central wards of Tokyo. One station to the left and you’re out of the city. Yet it is everything else then boring: Being one of the centers of counterculture since the 1960s, the are is home to a lot of independent businesses, artsy shops and high-end craftsmen. Near the train station you will see a large concentration of local pubs & bars, each and every one offering their own unique flavor. Chain-stores are a No-Go in these streets, and the area is staring to become popular among creative foreigners as well.

Asagaya

Located in the heart of Suginami Ward, Asagaya does not lack in the creative vibe either. The area is home to Tokyo’s Jazz scene, and there is Jazz festival called “Asagaya Jazz streets” held annually. While on the south exit you can find a traditional Japanese shopping street (known as Shotengai) with an abundance in restaurants, the north exit is home to large concentration of independently owned pubs, particularly in Tachi-nomi style (standing bars). Every pub has their own speciality, and you can find a variety of street-food here.

Koenji

It is a shame if you haven’t visited Koenji during a Tokyo trip (even though this applies to 95% of the people). Sometimes being referred to as the “India of Japan”, the town was center of Tokyo’s Hippie movement in the 1970s. Even today the town attracts Tokyo’s creatives (including Fumi, who lives here), and you can find tons of second-hand shops, unique cafes and live venues scattered around the area. The area is also not short on nightlife, so here you can find some of the best locals pubs and late-night food in the entire city.
